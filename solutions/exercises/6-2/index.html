    1. What is the significance of adding dopant atoms to an intrinsic semiconductor? Can two intrinsic semiconductors joined together make a diode?

      Intrinsic semiconductors have no impurities. Adding dopant atoms creates extra unbounded electrons/holes depending on the n/p dopant atom added. Impurity eigenstates appear and the level shifts (up/down for added donors/acceptors).

      To make a diode a p-n junction is needed (extrinsic semiconductors). Drawing a diagram is very helpful.

    2. Discuss which processes carry current in a diode under reverse bias.

      Under reverse bias only two processes carry out current: electrons that may be thermally excited into the conduction band (p-doped side) and holes that may be thermally excited into the valence band (n-doped side).

    7. What would be the advantage of doping the AlGaAs compared to the in this quantum well?

      If donor impurities are put outside of the well (on both sides, for example) the donated electrons can reduce their energies by falling into the well, but the ionized dopants remain behind. This gives an advantage because an extremely high mobility for electrons can be obtained within the quantum well (there are no ionized dopants in the well to scatter off of). This is called modulation doping.
